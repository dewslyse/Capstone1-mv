“Hello! Nature lovers” The International Biodiversity Conservation Summit 2022

A forum for conservation professionals and students, from over 60 countries, who are passionate about addressing biodiversity conservation and climate change challenges for a better future.

2022.03.24(Thu) ~ 25(Fri)

@ Trondheim Science Museum, Trondheim, Norway

Main Program

Lecture

Listen to speakers from various countries about their ongoing works in nature conservation.

Exhibition

Appreciate manuscripts and posters from experts and students in the field of conservation.

Forum

Have time to share your thoughts, opinions and questions with experts for each topic.

Workshop

Explore topics such as advance research, innovative approaches for management, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Network

Get opportunities to network with TICS affiliates around the world after the summit

Featured Speakers

Amanda Wheeler

Executive Director of the Trondheim Science Museum

Amanda is the Executive Director of the Trondheim Science Museum and also a fellow of the American Museum of Natural History. She is this year’s host of The International Biodiversity Conservation Summit.

Oliver Quist

Professor of Conservation Science, University of Cambridge

Oliver is the head of the Community Dynamics research area at the Centre for Biodiversity Dynamics (CBD). He has done extensive work in Conservation Biology. His current work focuses on the use of applied population dynamics for conservation and management.

Martha Roberts

Professor of Marine Sciences, Western University College

Martha is a biological oceanographer with strong interests in plankton ecology. Core questions of her current work has to do with how climate change (global warming and ocean acidification) affects plankton diversity and marine food webs.

Donald Ressler

Executive Director, International Biodiversity Conservation Group

Donald is the Executive Director of the International Biodiversity Conservation Group and a fellow of the American Museum of Natural History. He is the co-host of The International Biodiversity Conservation Summit.

Samantha Smith

Associate Professor of Ornithology, University of Twente

Samantha is the head of the lead researcher at the Centre for Ornithological Studies (COS). She has done extensive work on the world's avifauna. Her current work focuses on the use of applied population dynamics for conservation and management.

Ryan Merkley

Professor of Anthropology, University of Oslo

Ryan is a Anthropologist with interests in how social relations affect wildlife conservation and the utilization of natural resources. Core questions of his current work has to do with the human impacts and ramifications of global climate change.
