“Hello! Nature lovers” International Conservation Summit 2022

A forum for conservation professionals and students who are passionate about addressing biodiversity conservation and climate change challenges for a better future.

2022.03.24(Thu) ~ 25(Fri)

@ Trondheim Science Museum, Norway

Main Program

Lecture

Listen to speakers from various countries about their ongoing works in nature conservation.

Exhibition

Appreciate posters from experts and students in the field of conservation.

Forum

Have time to share your thoughts and opinions with experts for each topic.

Workshop

Explore topics such as advance research and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Network

Get opportunities to network with TICS affiliates around the world after the summit

Featured Speakers

Amanda Wheeler

Executive Director, Trondheim Science Museum

Amanda is the Executive Director of the Trondheim Science Museum. She is this year’s host of The International Biodiversity Conservation Summit.

Oliver Quist

Professor of Conservation Science, University of Cambridge

Oliver is the head of the Centre for Biodiversity Dynamics (CBD). His current work focuses on the use of applied population dynamics for conservation and management.

Martha Roberts

Professor of Marine Sciences, Western University College

Martha is a biological oceanographer with strong interests in plankton ecology. Her current work has to do with how climate change affects marine food webs.

Donald Ressler

Executive Director, International Biodiversity Conservation Group

Donald is a fellow of the American Museum of Natural History. He is the co-host of The International Biodiversity Conservation Summit.

Samantha Smith

Associate Professor of Ornithology, University of Twente

Samantha is the head of the lead researcher at the Centre for Ornithological Studies (COS). Her work focuses on the use of population dynamics for conservation.

Ryan Merkley

Professor of Anthropology, University of Oslo

Ryan is a Anthropologist with interests in how social relations affect wildlife conservation and the utilization of natural resources.
